CFD Analysis of Swirl Recovery in a Transonic Propeller

Problem Statement

Swirl losses in transonic propeller wakes impose a measurable efficiency penalty. The objective was to quantify swirl recovery through vane integration without simplified axisymmetric assumptions — preserving true 3D flow physics at transonic Mach regimes.

Analytical Approach

Parameterised 3D propeller and swirl vane models in CATIA V5. Structured mesh refinement in ANSYS Design Modeller resolved boundary-layer gradients and shock zones. Iterative BC calibration in Fluent & CFX achieved stable residual convergence across operating points.

Outcome

Validated performance dataset quantifying swirl recovery efficiency vs. vane angle-of-attack — yielding actionable design guidelines for in-service propulsion improvement.

Autonomous Mobile Robot with Dynamic Obstacle Avoidance

Problem Statement

Reliable autonomous navigation in unstructured environments demands real-time sensor fusion and path-following robust to dynamic obstacles — exposing the limits of sequential sensing and reactive-only control architectures.

Technical Integration

Arduino platform with ultrasonic sensors (forward detection) + infrared sensors (edge/lateral boundaries). Python state machine: classifies proximity, triggers evasive manoeuvres, re-engages primary trajectory with minimal deviation. Motor timing calibrated to sensor update latency for consistent avoidance across surface types.

Outcome

Demonstrated reliable autonomous navigation through variable-complexity obstacle courses. Modular, extensible codebase validated for waypoint-following and multi-robot coordination.
